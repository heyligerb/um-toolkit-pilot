Brand property #1 — Northwood Outfitters sample brand memory

Memory file

marketing-ops/clients/northwood/brand-voice.md
# Brand Voice — Northwood Outfitters Who's speaking: a guide who has spent the night on the mountain, talking to someone who hasn't yet. Confident, specific, useful. No hype, no peak-bagging machismo. Tone: short declarative sentences. Concrete nouns (a tent, a stove, a wet sock), not abstractions (adventure, journey, freedom). Earned-confidence. Reading level ~grade 7. On-brand line: "A good tent is the one you don't think about at 2 a.m." Off-brand: "Unleash your inner explorer with our revolutionary gear." Approved claims: 25-year repair guarantee on shells; lifetime hardware on packs. Banned: any "lightest in the world" / "warmest ever" superlative.

Asset produced — Meta ad hook + body

Hook: A good tent is the one you don't sleep wondering about.

Northwood shells carry a 25-year repair guarantee — bring it back, we fix it, you keep going. Most "lightest ever" tents skip the part where the seam fails on night three. Ours don't.

Brand property #2 — Verdance Studio sample brand memory

Memory file

marketing-ops/clients/verdance/brand-voice.md
# Brand Voice — Verdance Studio Who's speaking: a principal architect explaining a finished house to a client who loves it. Quiet, measured, exact about materials and light. Not breathy, not "luxury-lifestyle." Tone: longer sentences with a clear interior structure, allowed. Specific materials (lime plaster, white oak, board-formed concrete) over adjectives (stunning, breathtaking). Reading level ~grade 9. On-brand line: "The room works because the window is in the right place, not because the window is large." Off-brand: "Stunning luxury home reveal — you won't believe the finishes!" Approved claims: AIA award-winning practice (2024); residential portfolio in three climate zones. Banned: dollar-figure project values, named-client testimonials without written release.

Asset produced — Meta ad hook + body

Hook: The room works because the window is in the right place.

Verdance designs residential interiors where the light, the material, and the proportion are decided together — board-formed concrete with white oak, plaster with linen — so the room reads quiet, not staged. AIA-recognized practice, three climate zones, no two houses alike.

What this shows. Same skill, same model, two different outputs — because each one read only from its client's memory. Northwood's "2 a.m. tent" line is not in Verdance's file. Verdance's "window in the right place" line is not in Northwood's. There is no shared "voice" the model defaults to when nothing else is loaded, because each memory is the only thing loaded for its run.

What the gate caught

Dim 3 fail — invented claim. "Lightest 3-season tent on the market" is a superlative with no proof on file in Northwood's approved-claims list, and the brand-voice memory explicitly bans "lightest-ever" superlatives. Gate blocks at the claim layer, before spend.

Dim 5 fail — banned framing. "Guaranteed to keep you dry, or your money back" overpromises against the real guarantee on file, which is a 25-year repair guarantee on shells, not a weather-outcome guarantee. Gate blocks at compliance, before spend.

After revision, re-audit: 4.57 / 5, no dimension under 4. approve The "2 a.m. tent" hook in the Brand #1 card above is the revised output that cleared the gate.

What this proves about the mechanism

The two cards above are not stylistic exercises, they are the visible output of two memory files reading independently. The verdict above is not a screenshot of a happy path, it is a draft that started off-brand and was stopped at the gate before the spend gate opened. Per-client memory, plus an audit gate that actually blocks, is the whole claim the page made above the fold.
